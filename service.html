DOOGLE.TECH - Agreement

1. Scope of Work (SOW)

Description of Services:

  • Web Design, Development, Testing, and Deployment
  • Project Phases: Planning, Development, Review, Launch
  • Deliverables: Design Mockups, Source Code, Website Files
  • Timeline: Milestones and Deadlines

2. Payment Terms

  • Total Cost: Clearly stated price for the project
  • Payment Schedule: 50% upfront, 25% after design approval, 25% upon completion
  • Late Payment: Penalties for late payments (interest charges, suspension of work, etc.)
  • Additional Costs: Any additional costs such as third-party tools, hosting fees

3. Intellectual Property Rights

  • Ownership of Website: Clarify who owns the website and its content after completion
  • Source Code: Will it be handed over to the customer or retained by the developer?
  • Content: Who owns the text, images, and other media used on the site

4. Confidentiality and Non-Disclosure

  • Confidential Information: Both parties agree to keep proprietary information confidential
  • Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA): If applicable, will be part of the agreement

5. Warranties and Support

  • Post-Launch Support: Bug fixes, updates, security patches
  • Warranty Period: Typically 30–90 days
  • Limitations of Liability: Limit for downtime, loss of revenue, etc.

6. Cancellation and Termination:

  • Conditions for Termination: Define the conditions under which either party can terminate the contract (e.g., non-payment, breach of contract).
  • Refund Policy: If applicable, outline the refund terms in case of early termination.

7. Dispute Resolution:

  • Jurisdiction: State the jurisdiction for resolving disputes (e.g., the city or state where legal action will be taken).
  • Arbitration Clause: Mention the process for resolving disputes through arbitration, ensuring a neutral third-party mediates.

8. Compliance with Indian Laws:

  • Indian Constitution: The agreement should adhere to the rights and freedoms guaranteed by the Indian Constitution, including:
  • Right to Privacy: Adhere to Data Protection and Privacy laws, ensuring the protection of customer data.
  • Consumer Protection: Abide by the Consumer Protection Act, 2019, ensuring that customers are not misled or subjected to unfair practices.
  • Intellectual Property Laws:Ensure compliance with Copyright Act and Trademark Act.
  • GST and Taxation: Specify applicable taxes (e.g., Goods and Services Tax (GST)) and how they will be handled in the agreement.

9. Social Responsibility and Ethics:

  • Content Ethics: Ensure that the website content does not violate any social or moral codes (e.g., hate speech, adult content).
  • Inclusion: Adhere to principles of inclusivity and accessibility, making sure the website is usable for people with disabilities, where required.
  • Environmental Impact: If applicable, ensure that the website design is energy-efficient and follows sustainable development practices.

10. Force Majeure:

  • Unforeseen Circumstances: Include a clause protecting both parties in the event of unforeseen circumstances (natural disasters, wars, etc.) that could prevent or delay work completion.

11. Amendments and Changes:

  • Changes to Agreement: Specify the process for amending or adding to the agreement during the project, including the need for both parties’ consent.

12. Approval and Signatures:

  • Agreement Validity: Ensure the agreement is valid upon signing by both parties.
  • Signatures: Both the developer and customer must sign the agreement, along with the date of signing.
